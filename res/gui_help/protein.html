Protein

Proteins are large biomolecules with complex structures that are constructed using RNA. Despite the complexity, a protein is just a sequence of amino acids. Its structure and function are determined by this sequence. Each amino acid is made from a one or more different codons. A codon is simply just a sequence of three bases. Each amino acid may be the result one, none, or many codons. Codons that don't correspond to an amino acid are called stop codons, which terminate the sequence.

Example:

Sequence Type Sequence
DNA GCTACTAGGTCA
RNA GCUACUAGGUCA
Protein ATRS
